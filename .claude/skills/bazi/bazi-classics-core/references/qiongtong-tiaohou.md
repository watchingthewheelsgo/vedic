# 穷通宝鉴：调候层

Use this reference in Step 2.

## Purpose

《穷通宝鉴》 answers a narrower question than modern “喜用神” summaries:

> Given a day stem born in a month, what climate problem must be treated first?

Treat its conclusion as **调候用神**. It may agree with, conflict with, or condition the 格局用神 from 《子平真诠》.

## Audit Questions

1. What is the day stem?
2. What is the month branch by solar term?
3. Is the month cold, hot, dry, wet, early-season, mid-season, or late-season?
4. What is the primary medicine: warming, cooling, moistening, drying, cutting, cultivating, draining, or protecting?
5. Is the medicine visible and usable:透干、通根、得令、被合、被冲、被克、隔位?
6. Does the chart have the opposite disease: too much medicine, no medicine, medicine damaged, or medicine present but blocked?

## Core Rules

- Spring wood often needs warmth first, then moisture; too much water at early spring can damage growth.
- Summer wood often needs water to moisten; excessive fire can burn it; some metal can shape it, but heavy metal harms if the wood is weak.
- Autumn wood often faces metal pressure; fire may be needed to temper metal, while water can nourish or over-float depending on timing.
- Winter wood often needs fire warmth and earth cultivation; too much water can make wood drift.
- Summer fire is not automatically good for fire day masters; excess heat may need water and wet earth.
- Winter water is not automatically good for water day masters; cold water often needs fire warmth.
- Metal in winter may sink in cold water; fire can be required to warm and refine.
- Earth has no fixed seasonal nature; judge by whether it is cold, wet, dry, scorched, loose, or blocked.

## Pitfalls

- Do not say “缺火所以补火” unless the seasonal disease actually requires fire.
- Do not overwrite 格局用神. A chart can need fire for warmth while using官印/财食 structurally.
- Do not treat a hidden medicine as equal to a clear, rooted, protected medicine.
- Do not ignore excess medicine. Too much water, fire, metal, wood, or earth can become the disease.
